The bar graph illustrates the Reserves and Surplus held by EIH Limited, expressed in millions of Indian Rupees, over a five-year period from FY 2018-19 to FY 2022-23. Starting at ₹27,408 million in FY 2018-19, the company's Reserves and Surplus witnessed a growth of 2.3% reaching ₹28,049 million in FY 2019-20. This was followed by a marginal decrease to ₹28,006 million in FY 2020-21. However, FY 2021-22 saw a more noticeable dip to ₹26,832 million, indicating a potential utilization of reserves during that year. Despite this dip, the company rebounded strongly in FY 2022-23, achieving ₹29,931 million in Reserves and Surplus, marking an 11.6% increase from the previous year and surpassing the figures from all preceding years. This significant growth in FY 2022-23 can be attributed to the company's strong financial performance and profitability during the year, allowing it to bolster its reserves. Over the five-year period, EIH Limited achieved a CAGR of 1.8% in Reserves and Surplus.
Overall Summary:
EIH Limited's Reserves and Surplus trend indicates a financially prudent approach, even in the face of economic challenges. Despite a dip in FY 2021-22, likely due to the impact of the pandemic, the company demonstrated a strong recovery in FY 2022-23, exceeding pre-pandemic levels. This positive trend, coupled with a 1.8% 5-year CAGR, highlights EIH Limited's ability to generate and retain profits, reinforcing its financial stability and capacity to navigate future uncertainties.
