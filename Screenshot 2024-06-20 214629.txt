The bar graph presents the Return on Equity (ROE) for Eicher Motors over four financial years. In FY 2019-20, the company achieved a strong ROE of 24.8%. However, this figure declined significantly to 15.1% in FY 2020-21. While there was a slight improvement in FY 2021-22, with ROE reaching 15.4%, it remained significantly lower than the pre-pandemic level. Eicher Motors witnessed a strong recovery in FY 2022-23, with ROE bouncing back to 22.2%, surpassing the figures for FY 2020-21 and FY 2021-22.
Overall Summary: 
Eicher Motors' Return on Equity reflects a volatile trend, with a sharp decline in FY 2020-21 followed by a gradual recovery. The decline can be attributed to the pandemic's impact on the global economy and the automotive sector. However, the strong rebound in FY 2022-23, exceeding pre-pandemic levels, suggests successful strategic decisions, improved operational efficiency, and a more favorable market environment. This positive trend in ROE indicates a healthy return for investors and signifies the company's potential for future growth and profitability. The note mentioning no political contributions in FY 2022-23 aims to provide transparency and address potential concerns regarding the company's financial performance being influenced by non-business factors.
