The bar graph showcases the Basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) for Eicher Motors over four consecutive financial years. In FY 2019-20, the EPS stood at Rs. 69.75. This figure dropped significantly to Rs. 48.68 in FY 2020-21, representing a decrease of approximately 30.2%. However, the EPS recovered in FY 2021-22, reaching Rs. 58.02, marking an increase of about 19.2% compared to the previous year. Eicher Motors witnessed a substantial surge in EPS in FY 2022-23, reaching Rs. 95.91, a significant jump of about 65.3% from FY 2021-22.
Overall Summary: 
Eicher Motors' Basic EPS demonstrates volatility over the four years, with a significant dip in FY 2020-21, likely due to the pandemic's impact on the global economy and the automotive sector. However, the strong recovery and substantial growth in FY 2022-23 signify a positive turnaround for the company. This impressive EPS growth can be attributed to a combination of factors like increased profitability, potentially driven by higher sales, improved operational efficiency, and successful strategic initiatives. This positive trend in EPS bodes well for investors, indicating potentially higher shareholder returns in the future.
